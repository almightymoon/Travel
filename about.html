Roam started from a frustration: the best trips were never the ones with the thickest itineraries. They were the ones with great hosts, a stay you didn’t want to leave, and enough open space for the day to surprise you.

We keep groups small — usually eight to twelve — so the journey still feels human. Local hosts shape the rhythm. We handle the soft infrastructure: arrivals that don’t rush you, tables that actually seat everyone, and routes that leave room to wander.

Twelve years on, the rule hasn’t changed: if it feels like a checklist, we cut it. If it feels like a memory forming, we keep it.
